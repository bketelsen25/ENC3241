# Instructions

How to Construct an Outdoor Stone Firepit with Stone-Leg Benches

Introduction

These instructions explain how to construct a circular outdoor firepit using stone bricks and four
surrounding benches with stone brick legs and wooden beam seats. The guide covers the
planning and construction steps needed to build a durable firepit seating area suitable for public
or personal use.

---

Caution Notices

Ensure that all wooden bench beams are far enough from the firepit opening to avoid heat
damage or ignition.

Cities may have firepit regulations. Always verify local fire safety ordinances before constructing
or using your firepit.

---

Step-by-Step Construction

Preparing the Site

Step 1: Select an open section of land that is at least 10 - 20 ft away from structures, fences, or
trees.

Step 2: Mark a circular area for the firepit, approximately 3 - 4 ft in diameter.

Step 3: Dig down 6 - 8 inches within the circle to create a stable base for the firepit.

Step 4: Fill the base with gravel, spreading 2 - 3 inches evenly across the excavated area to
assist drainage.

---

Building the Circular Firepit

Step 1: Place the first ring of stone bricks, arranging around the edge of the circular base,
ensuring they form a smooth circle and sit level with each other.

Step 2: Check alignment by using a level across multiple bricks to confirm the entire ring is
even.

Step 3: Add the second layer of bricks, stacking them staggered over the seams of the first layer
to increase stability.

Step 4: Continue stacking bricks until the firepit reaches 12 - 18 inches high.

---

Constructing the Stone Bench Supports

Step 1: Measure four seating positions around the firepit, spacing the benches evenly so each
bench faces the firepit.

Step 2: Build the first bench leg using stone bricks, stacking them vertically until the structure
reaches 16 - 18 inches tall.

Step 3: Construct a second leg parallel to the first, placing it 3 - 5 ft away.

Step 4: Repeat the process for the remaining three benches, two legs per bench.

---

Installing the Wooden Bench Seats

Step 1: Place a wooden beam across each pair of stone supports, centering it so it rests evenly
on both stone legs.

Step 2: Secure the beams using outdoor construction adhesive to prevent the beam from
shifting.

Step 3: Check the seating height and stability by sitting gently on each bench to verify that the
beams remain level and secure.

Step 4: Adjust alignment if necessary, making small shifts or adjusting the stone supports until
stable if a beam rocks or tilts.
